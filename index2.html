Part 1: The Base (Walk around the outside)
1. The Boy & The Tiger
FIND

The statue of a boy fighting a lion (or tiger).

THE STORY

A teacher yelled "Poy Sala!" (Strike, Sala!) to this boy. He fought the tiger, won, and started the Hoysala dynasty. It's like their superhero logo!

2. The Weight Lifters
FIND

The very bottom row of the temple walls. It is a long line of elephants.

CHALLENGE

Walk along the row. Can you find two elephants that look exactly the same? (Spoiler: The artists made every single one different!)

FACT

There are 650 elephants holding up the temple.

3. The Mash-up Monster (Makara)
FIND

A creature with a Crocodile's mouth, an Elephant's trunk, a Lion's paws, and a Peacock's tail.

CLUE

It's usually found near the elephants or doorways. It represents the power of water.

4. The War Horses
FIND

The row of carvings showing horses ready for battle.

LOOK CLOSER

Look at the riders' feet. They are using stirrups and boots‚Äîtech that was very advanced for that time!

5. The Lion Faces
FIND

The row of scary lion faces staring at you.

COUNT

How many can you count in 30 seconds?

Part 2: The Stone People (Look up at the angled statues)
6. The Selfie Queen (Darpana Sundari)
FIND

The most famous lady here. She is holding a mirror and checking her style.

DETAIL

Notice how she stands. Is she stiff like a robot, or relaxed?

SCIENCE

The stone (Soapstone) is soft when dug up but turns hard as steel over time!

7. The Monkey Attack!
FIND

A lady looking annoyed because a mischievous monkey is pulling her saree! She is trying to shoo it away with a branch.

FUN FACT

Even 900 years ago, monkeys were causing trouble!

8. The Parrot Whisperer
FIND

A lady with a parrot sitting on her arm. She is talking to it.

IMPOSSIBLE STONE

The bangles on her wrist are carved so perfectly that (if you were allowed to touch them) they could actually spin around her arm!

9. The Huntress
FIND

A lady holding a bow and arrow. She isn't dancing; she is aiming at a target.

VIBE

She's the ancient version of Katniss Everdeen.

10. The Lizard Surprise
SUPER CHALLENGE

Look at the back of the "Mirror Lady" or the figures right next to her. Can you spot a lizard carved on a fruit or leaf?

LEGEND

It's a secret "easter egg" left by the artist.

Part 3: Inside the Mystery Hall
11. The Spotlight Pillar (Mohini Pillar)
FIND

The pillar that is incredibly smooth, black, and shiny.

TEST

Stand near it. Can you see your own reflection in the stone?

FACT

No modern machine has been able to polish stone this well!

12. The Spinner (Narasimha Pillar)
FIND

The pillar covered in tiny, detailed red/brown carvings.

SECRET

This pillar has ball bearings inside. Long ago, people could rotate this huge stone pillar with just one finger!

13. The Gravity Defier
FIND

A tall pillar (either in the courtyard or the hall) that stands on only three corners.

THE TRICK

You can slide a piece of paper or a handkerchief under the fourth corner. It's hanging in the air!

14. The Giant Kaleidoscope
FIND

Stand in the dead center of the hall and look Straight Up.

DESCRIBE IT

It looks like a giant inverted flower.

NAME

This is the Bhuvaneshwari ceiling.

15. The Angry Avatar
FIND

A carving of a god ripping open a demon. This is Narasimha (Half-Lion, Half-Man).

MOOD

Most statues here are smiling, but this one is fierce!

Part 4: Tiny Details (For Eagle Eyes)
16. The Fly on the Corn
FIND

Look for a small statue of a lady holding a corn cob.

ZOOM IN

If you have 20/20 vision, spot the tiny housefly sitting on the corn kernel.

WOW

Imagine carving a fly out of rock without breaking it!

17. The Artist's Autograph
FIND

Look at the feet of the statues. Can you see squiggly writing?

FACT

Those are signatures! The artists (like "Dasoja" or "Chavana") signed their work, just like painters do today.

18. The Skull Necklace
FIND

A warrior or guardian wearing a necklace made of skulls.

VIBE

A bit spooky, right?

19. The Food Chain
FIND

A carving showing a snake eating a frog, while a peacock eats the snake...

LESSON

It shows the circle of life.

20. The Frog Temple
FIND

Go to the smaller temple behind the main one. It is named Kappe Chennigaraya.

TRANSLATION

"Kappe" means Frog in Kannada! Legend says a frog was found alive inside a stone here.

üéØ Did You Know? (Fun Facts to Share)

No Cement: The whole temple is held together by weight and balance. No glue, no mortar!

103 Years: That's how long it took to build. If they started in 2025, they wouldn't finish until the year 2128!

Stolen Designs: The jewelry carved on the statues is so accurate that jewelers today use photos of these statues to make real gold necklaces!
